# System vs Device vs Tool vs Asset
## StoryForge101 Guide

This guide helps you control **agency, control, and inevitability** in a story.

You are not naming things.
You are deciding **what can be touched, what must be endured, and what keeps working without you.**

---

## Core Definitions

### System
Something you are inside.
You do not pick it up.
You navigate it, endure it, or reshape it slowly.

Examples:
- society
- economy
- global healthcare
- magic rules of a world

**Feel:** ambient, enclosing, hard to escape

---

### Device
Something constructed that does something.
It exists whether you use it or not.

Examples:
- a trap
- an algorithm
- a plot device
- a policy mechanism

**Feel:** intentional, designed, acting on the world

---

### Tool
A device you are actively using.

Examples:
- a weapon in hand
- a spell being cast
- a prompt being written

**Feel:** direct control, skill, responsibility

---

### Asset
Something placed into the world that keeps producing effects without you holding it.

Examples:
- a reputation
- a network
- a published document
- a trap already set
- a bug report propagating through systems

**Feel:** persistent leverage, background influence

---

## Relationship States

Same object, different state:

- device → exists  
- tool → in your hand  
- asset → working for you without you  

You are not changing the object.
You are changing your **relationship to it**.

---

## When to Use Each

### Use System when you want:
- pressure without a clear target  
- characters to adapt or survive  
- slow change or large-scale struggle  

> "you cannot fight it directly"

---

### Use Device when you want:
- intention to be visible  
- something that can be targeted  
- a mechanism behind outcomes  

> "this is causing it"

---

### Use Tool when you want:
- immediate action  
- skill expression  
- responsibility and consequence  

> "I am doing this"

---

### Use Asset when you want:
- delayed impact  
- indirect influence  
- leverage across time or distance  

> "this keeps working even when I'm not"

---

## Steering the Story

### If your story feels stuck:
- too many Systems → give a Device to target  
- too many Devices → give a Tool to act with  
- too many Tools → convert one into an Asset for persistence  

---

### If stakes feel weak:
- turn a Tool into a System (loss of control)  
- turn an Asset into a Device (make it visible and attackable)

---

### If pacing is off:
- Tools → fast  
- Devices → medium  
- Assets → delayed  
- Systems → slow  

Mix them deliberately.

---

## Hidden Power Moves

### Reclassification

Change what something *is* in the story:

- System → Device → now it can be attacked  
- Device → Tool → now it can be used  
- Tool → Asset → now it persists  
- Asset → System → now it surrounds everyone  

Same thing. Different role. Different story.

---

### Audience Control

What the audience feels depends on classification:

- System → inevitability  
- Device → causality  
- Tool → agency  
- Asset → strategy  

---

## Quick Reference

- System = you are inside it  
- Device = it exists and acts  
- Tool = you are using it  
- Asset = it keeps working without you  

---

## Final Rule

You are not describing the world.

You are deciding:

- what can be touched  
- what can be changed  
- what keeps working  

Choose carefully.

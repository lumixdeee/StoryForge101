# Archive of "Received" or Spirit-Attributed Constructed Languages

A survey of languages whose creators claimed they were **received from non-human or transcendent sources** rather than deliberately invented.

These sources include angels, spirits, aliens, dream entities, trance states, psychedelic visions, or divine revelation.

This document maps major examples plus a larger archive of lesser-known cases drawn from occult history, séance literature, religious movements, and modern psychonaut reports.

---

# 1. Angelic and Visionary Languages

## Enochian
- Source: Angelic revelation during scrying sessions
- Receiver: John Dee and Edward Kelley (1580s)
- Description: Vocabulary and magical invocations allegedly dictated by angels.
- Link: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enochian
- Notes: One of the most elaborate "received languages" with script, grammar fragments, and ritual texts.

## Lingua Ignota
- Source: Mystical visions
- Receiver: Hildegard of Bingen (12th century)
- Description: Sacred vocabulary used in devotional writings.
- Link: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lingua_ignota

## Angelic Script Systems
Renaissance occult alphabets sometimes treated as celestial writing systems.

Examples:
- Celestial Alphabet  
  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celestial_Alphabet
- Malachim script
- Transitus Fluvii
- Theban alphabet

These often appear in grimoires and magical manuscripts.

# 2. Spirit Medium Languages (19th–20th Century)

## Martian Language
- Source: Spirit medium trance communications
- Medium: Hélène Smith
- Period: 1890s
- Description: Claimed language of Martian civilization.
- Link: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/H%C3%A9l%C3%A8ne_Smith

Studied by psychologist Theodore Flournoy in *From India to the Planet Mars* (1900).

## Ultra-Martian
- Source: Later séances of Hélène Smith
- Description: Related language from a second Martian civilization.

## Selenite Language
- Source: Same medium
- Description: Claimed Moon language communicated through trance writing.

# 3. Occult and Magical Ritual Languages

These languages appear in grimoires or magical systems and are often treated as **divine or primordial speech**.

## Barbarous Names
- Source: Greco-Egyptian magical papyri
- Description: Long ritual utterances believed to invoke divine beings.
- Example texts: Greek Magical Papyri (PGM)

## Enochian Magic Corpus
- Includes:
  - Liber Loagaeth
  - Angelic Calls (Keys)
  - Enochian Tablets
- Context: Renaissance ceremonial magic

Reference:
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enochian_magic

# 4. Religious or Prophetic Languages

## Glossolalia Systems
- Source: Pentecostal and Charismatic Christian practice
- Description: "Speaking in tongues"
- Some communities develop semi-stable vocabularies.

Overview:
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossolalia

## Mormon Script Experiments
Early Latter-day Saint movements explored sacred alphabets and linguistic reform.

Example:
- Deseret Alphabet  
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deseret_alphabet

While not "received speech", it was framed as restoring purity to language.

# 6. Dream-Revealed Languages

These are often private languages reported in dream journals.

Examples documented in linguistics and psychology:

## The "Dream Language" Journals
Individuals recording structured languages appearing in dreams.

Common characteristics:
- simple phonologies
- repetitive syllable structures
- small lexicons

Academic references appear in studies of **private languages and glossolalia**.

# 10. Structural Patterns Observed Across Received Languages

Across the archive, these languages show recurring properties:

### Native Language Influence
Phonology and grammar often resemble the creator's native language.

### Limited Corpus
Many have small vocabularies or only ritual phrases.

### High Symbolic Density
Used in:
- magical invocation
- spiritual communication
- mythic storytelling

### Script Creation
Many include invented alphabets.

### Transmission Context
Typically produced during:
- trance
- meditation
- ritual
- dreams
- psychedelic experiences

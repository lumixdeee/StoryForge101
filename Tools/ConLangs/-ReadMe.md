## Received Language Archive

This directory collects and maps **languages claimed to be received rather than invented**.

In most conlang communities a language normally falls into one of three categories:

* naturally acquired languages (family, culture, region)
* deliberately constructed languages
* fictional narrative languages

This archive focuses on a different and stranger category:
**languages whose creators insist they were given to them by some external source.**

Examples of these sources include:

* angels
* spirits
* extraterrestrials
* dream entities
* trance states
* psychedelic experiences
* mystical revelation
* ritual or magical practice

These languages appear across history in religious visions, occult grimoires, séance transcripts, UFO contact movements, shamanic traditions, and modern psychonaut reports.

The purpose of this archive is **not to judge the claims**, but simply to **map and catalogue the phenomenon**.

It turns out there are **far more examples than most people realize**.

# Why This Exists

The phenomenon of **"received languages"** sits at an unusual intersection of several fields:

* linguistics
* religious history
* occult traditions
* anthropology
* psychology
* science fiction
* worldbuilding

Despite appearing repeatedly across centuries, the examples are scattered across many different archives and disciplines.

This project attempts to **bring them into one place**.
